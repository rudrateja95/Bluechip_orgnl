Acadamic Training

  • The Emergency Medical Responder Instructor Course (EMRIC) is designed to equip individuals with the skills necessary to teach emergency medical response techniques to others. It focuses on building foundational knowledge in medical emergencies, providing instructors with both the technical expertise and teaching strategies to effectively train responders in life-saving procedures.

  • The EMRIC curriculum covers a broad range of topics essential for emergency medical care. This includes patient assessment, airway management, CPR, trauma response, and dealing with medical emergencies like heart attacks, strokes, and diabetic crises. Instructors are trained not only on the procedures themselves but also on how to effectively communicate and demonstrate these skills to students in a classroom or practical setting.

  • Upon completion, participants earn certification as Emergency Medical Responder Instructors, allowing them to teach courses like basic life support (BLS) or first aid. This course is ideal for healthcare professionals, firefighters, law enforcement officers, or anyone looking to advance their career in emergency services by becoming a certified instructor. The training emphasizes hands-on practice, ensuring that instructors can confidently guide students in real-world emergency scenarios.
